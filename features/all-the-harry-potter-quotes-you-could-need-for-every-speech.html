    All the Harry Potter quotes you could need for every kind of speech

    Published on Jun 18th 2025
    WritingWriting a speech is a daunting task. If you’re in need of some inspiration, the Harry Potter stories are full of wonderful words for all occasions. Whether you want a witty one-liner or poignant pearl of wisdom, here are all the quotes you could need for weddings, birthday parties and beyond...

    That perfect opening line

    First impressions are crucial when it comes to speeches. People aren’t going to hear all the wonderful things you have to say if they’re not hooked from the very beginning. If you’re in need of a dazzling opener, we think these will do just the trick…

    ‘Words are, in my not-so-humble opinion, our most inexhaustible source of magic’ – Albus Dumbledore
    Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows Part Two

    If there was one character that knew how to weave words into a beautiful tapestry, it was Albus Dumbledore. Kicking things off with a poignant quote by him is sure to capture people’s attention.

    ‘I sometimes find, and I am sure you know the feeling, that I simply have too many thoughts and memories crammed into my mind.’ – Albus Dumbledore
    Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire

    Another gem from Dumbledore, opening with this very relatable feeling will allow your audience to connect to you straightaway.

    ‘I solemnly swear that I am up to no good’
    Harry Potter and the Prisoner of Azkaban

    Why not start a more light-hearted speech with the words used to reveal the secrets of the Marauder’s Map? People would instantly know that fun and mischief were ahead.

    ‘Before we begin our banquet, I would like to say a few words. And here they are: Nitwit! Blubber! Oddment! Tweak!’ – Albus Dumbledore
    Harry Potter and the Philosopher’s Stone

    Sometimes you just have to go big or go home. Using this eccentric Dumbledore quote guarantees people are going to take notice.

    How to sign off

    There’s nothing worse than a speech which just tails off awkwardly. You need a definite end. Something that is meaningful, memorable and has style.

    ‘Mischief managed’
    Harry Potter and the Prisoner of Azkaban

    The words used to wipe the Marauder’s Map would be the ideal way to close a best man or bridesmaid speech (especially if used as a bookend to a Marauder’s Map themed opening line).

    ‘All was well.’
    Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows

    Nothing says ‘the end’ like the final line of the final Harry Potter book! A wonderfully sentimental way to wrap things up.

    Wedding

    No wedding is complete without a good speech – emphasis being on good. In fact, there are often several speeches, and you don’t want yours to be the damp squib. Use one of these quotes to help prevent that awkward outcome.

    ‘Age is foolish and forgetful when it underestimates youth’ – Albus Dumbledore
    Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince

    This might not instantly scream wedding, but we think it could be a lovely inclusion for a couple who were once childhood sweethearts.

    ‘It is impossible to manufacture or imitate love.’ – Horace Slughorn
    Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince

    Slughorn might have been talking about Amortentia here, but this would be wonderful used as a reference to the obvious and visible love between a couple.

    ‘You are protected, in short, by your ability to love!’ – Albus Dumbledore
    Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince

    This would be a great line to use when talking about how the strength of the newlyweds love and how it will help to protect them during hard times.

    ‘There are some things you can't share without ending up liking each other, and knocking out a twelve-foot mountain troll is one of them.’
    Harry Potter and the Philosopher’s Stone

    One for a bridesmaid or best man speech, while we doubt you have actually fought a troll together, it would be a great way to tee up a hilarious anecdote.

    ‘Oh, to be young and to feel love's keen sting.’ – Albus Dumbledore
    Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince

    A light-hearted way to reference the depth of love between the couple.

    ‘But I am the Chosen One.’
    Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince

    Commitment is serious… but it doesn’t mean your vows can’t be fun. The perfect line for a fun-loving pair – especially if delivered with some of Harry’s sass.

    Birthday

    A major milestone, birthdays are both a time of celebration and reflection. We think these quotes capture that sentiment beautifully.

    ‘It matters not what someone is born, but what they grow to be!’ – Albus Dumbledore
    Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire

    A fitting quote for someone reaching a significant birthday, especially if you went on to talk about all the brilliant things they have accomplished.

    ‘It is a strange thing, but when you are dreading something, and would give anything to slow down time, it has a disobliging habit of speeding up.’
    Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire

    Bear with us here, while this quote might seem a little doom and gloom, we can imagine it being used by a parent to highlight how wonderful it has been to watch their child grow.

    ‘Time is making fools of us again.’ – Albus Dumbledore
    Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince

    Or this could be an alternative, if used to show how time flies when you’re having fun.

    ‘Wit beyond measure is man's greatest treasure.’ – Luna Lovegood
    Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix

    Engraved on Ravenclaw’s diadem, using this quote to illustrate the intelligence and humour of the birthday person can only end well.

    ‘Yeah, size is no guarantee of power’ – George Weasley
    Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix

    You could easily use this for a speech that talks about how someone has always been a force of nature worth celebrating.

    ‘One can never have enough socks.’ – Albus Dumbledore
    Harry Potter and the Philosopher’s Stone

    If there’s one gift you can rely on receiving every year, it’s socks. Keep this quote in your back pocket if you decide you want to say a few words of thanks at your party.

    New adventures

    Life is a journey, one packed full of action and change. These quotes are ideal for when you want to commemorate new beginnings – whether that’s a new job, travelling the world, a baby shower or even a graduation.

    ‘It is our choices, Harry, that show what we truly are, far more than our abilities.’ – Albus Dumbledore
    Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets

    Graduations are all about moving forward. This quote is a poignant reminder that the world is at your fingertips and it’s up to you to decide what you want your legacy to be.

    ‘Books! And cleverness! There are more important things – friendship and bravery’ – Hermione Granger
    Harry Potter and the Philosopher’s Stone

    In a similar vein, this would be a great alternative to the previous quote – conveying that success isn’t just academic, it’s also shown in how you choose to live your life.

    ‘You sort of start thinking anything's possible if you've got enough nerve’ – Ginny Weasley
    Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix

    Any life change requires courage, as summed up in this quote from Ginny Weasley. One that would be good to use in your farewell speech – offering insight into your decision.

    ‘Let us step out into the night and pursue that flighty temptress, adventure’ – Albus Dumbledore
    Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince

    A versatile quote that can be used for all sorts of speeches related to an exciting life change.

    ‘What’s life without a little risk?’ – Sirius Black
    Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix

    Add a bit of Sirius’ devil-may-care attitude to your speech – your audience will soon share your enthusiasm.

    ‘Where your treasure is, there will your heart be also.’
    Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows

    Ok, this quote might have been engraved on Kendra and Ariana Dumbledore’s tombstone, but the message is one worth sharing on happier occasions too – such as at a baby shower.

    ‘It does not do to dwell on dreams and forget to live, remember that.’ – Albus Dumbledore
    Harry Potter and the Philosopher’s Stone

    These words of wisdom from Dumbledore would bolster any speech that focuses on seizing the day.

    ‘Don’t let the Muggles get you down.’ – Ron Weasley
    Harry Potter and the Prisoner of Azkaban

    An excellent way to end a speech sending someone off one their next adventure. For every new challenge, there are new obstacles – but they’ve got this.

    Funeral

    Navigating loss is never easy – how can you put something like that into words? Words that will bring comfort to those left behind and encourage them to keep going. Some of these beautiful lines from Harry Potter might just help.

    ‘To the well-organised mind, death is but the next great adventure.’ – Albus Dumbledore
    Harry Potter and the Philosopher’s Stone

    While death feels final, for many – especially those who have lived a long life – it is seen as the next step of their journey.

    ‘You think the dead we have loved ever truly leave us? You think that we don’t recall them more clearly than ever in times of great trouble?’ – Albus Dumbledore
    Harry Potter and the Prisoner of Azkaban

    This quote could be used as a comforting reminder that we are never truly gone – as long as we remain the hearts and memories of those who loved us. In that sense, they’re always with us.

    ‘Things we lose have a way of coming back to us in the end, if not always in the way we expect.’ – Luna Lovegood
    Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix

    This insight from Luna Lovegood could be used as an alternative to convey the same message.

    ‘It is the unknown we fear when we look upon death and darkness, nothing more.’ – Albus Dumbledore
    Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince

    Death is such a huge concept to reckon with and that can be terrifying. This quote could be used as a soothing balm in your speech – allowing people to gain perspective.

    ‘Do not pity the dead, Harry. Pity the living, and, above all, those who live without love.’ – Albus Dumbledore
    Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows

    While we mourn those who have left us, life keeps going. You could use this quote to encourage people to hold on to one another and make the most of every moment.

    ‘But you know, happiness can be found even in the darkest of times, if one only remembers to turn on the light.’ – Albus Dumbledore
    Harry Potter and the Prisoner of Azkaban

    Finally, we have these words from Dumbledore that speak for themselves. A beautiful message of hope.
